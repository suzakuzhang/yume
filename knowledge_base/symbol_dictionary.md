# 跨传统统一符号词典 / Unified Dream-Symbol Dictionary

> Generated from `symbol_dictionary.json` — every symbol's readings across all seven dossiers (Freud · Jung · Post-Freudian · Chinese 周公解梦 · Divination · Dream Science · Philosophy). Use as a SEED for each agent's Phase-A hypothesis, never a verdict; Jung must amplify, not look up; Dream Science readings are common associations, not determinations.

## Water / sea / flood / 水

- **Freud**: A birth symbol: emerging from or being rescued from water = being born; entering or plunging in figures birth and the relation to the uterine waters.
- **Jung**: The unconscious itself, the maternal collective depths; drowning = being overwhelmed by unconscious contents, diving = willing descent.
- **Post-Freudian**: Bachelard-via-Hillman: a material image of dissolution and the fluid self; Boss would ask what fluid or engulfing world this discloses.
- **Chinese 周公解梦**: 多吉: 水上行者主大吉、大水澄清大吉祥、自在水中大吉利;唯『水上立者主凶事』。《灵枢》以梦涉大水为阴气盛/恐惧之病梦。
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: Element of Cups/Water and the Moon: emotional and unconscious depths; a flood = feelings overwhelming the ego. Cards: The Moon (XVIII), The Star (XVII).
- **Dream Science**: No fixed meaning; via continuity hypothesis, recurrent water imagery mirrors the dreamer's waking emotional state rather than a coded message.
- **Philosophy**: Bachelard's element of dissolution and fluid identity, where fixed edges blur — echoing Zhuangzi's flux; read as material reverie, not a fixed code.

## Fire / flames / burning / 火

- **Freud**: Associated with urinary/erotic excitation; in the fire-myth discussions, sexual desire — flames carry the heat of forbidden passion.
- **Jung**: Libido as psychic energy, transformation, purification; the alchemical agent of change. Destructive fire can compensate an over-controlled, frozen attitude.
- **Chinese 周公解梦**: 总体主兴旺发财:『火焰炎炎主发财、火烧自是主兴旺、身在火中贵人扶』;但『火从地生疾病至、火烟黑色主疾病』为凶。《灵枢》另解为阳气盛之病梦。八卦类象→离。
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: Element of Wands/Fire: will, spirit, creative or sexual energy; The Tower's destructive awakening or Aces of Wands. Alchemically the calcinatio burning away the inessential.
- **Dream Science**: Treated as content to be coded, not symbolized; recurrence is read through continuity against waking concerns, never as omen.
- **Philosophy**: Bachelard (Psychoanalysis of Fire): an ambivalent image of transformation and desire that both creates and consumes; material reverie rather than a fixed emotion.

## Snake / serpent / 蛇

- **Freud**: 'The most important symbol of the male member' — a prime phallic symbol; its form and movement represent the penis and male sexuality.
- **Jung**: Instinctual, chthonic energy and transformation; threshold-guardian of the unconscious; renewal (shedding skin) or a Shadow encounter.
- **Chinese 周公解梦**: 多主财与贵子:『蛇咬人主得大财、蛇入怀中生贵子、蛇绕身者生贵子、龙蛇入门主得财』;女子梦蛇为生女之祥(象梦『维虺维蛇,女子之祥』)。
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: Healing, wisdom, kundalini/transformative energy; the ouroboros (death-rebirth cycle); the 'Serpent of Wisdom' winding the Tree-of-Life paths. Ambivalent: poison or medicine.
- **Dream Science**: No validated universal meaning; coded as an animal character and read for continuity with waking concerns.
- **Philosophy**: A test case for the report problem: note how vividly it was 'seen' vs. inferred; meaning emerges from the dreamer's resonance (Bachelard's reverberation), not a universal code.

## Falling / 坠落 / 堕

- **Freud**: Often yielding to (sexual) temptation — moral/erotic surrender, especially in women; also draws on anxiety and loss of control.
- **Jung**: Loss of the ego's inflated/over-controlled standpoint; a compensatory pull back toward the ground/unconscious; an invitation to relinquish a one-sided height.
- **Post-Freudian**: Hartmann: a Central Image of vulnerability/loss of control. Adler: a feared loss of status within the life-style. Gestalt: identify with the falling itself to own the surrendered control.
- **Chinese 周公解梦**: 《灵枢·淫邪发梦》『下盛则梦堕』——身体下部气盛、阴阳失衡的病梦信号;传统亦视坠落为失位、厄运之兆。
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: Loss of structure, Saturn-ground giving way; The Tower (XVI) — collapse of false foundations preceding renewal; often the nigredo descent.
- **Dream Science**: Among the most prevalent typical dreams (~74%; Nielsen). Some link it to spontaneous vestibular (balance) reactivation during REM rather than symbolic 'loss of control'; continuity associates frequency with insecurity/stress but universal-meaning evidence is weak.
- **Philosophy**: Binswanger/Foucault: a downward existential trajectory toward loss of ground or freedom; phenomenologically the lived body's loss of orientation, not an omen.

## Flying / floating / 飞 / 飞行

- **Freud**: A symbol of sexual excitement and erection — flying/floating dreams represent erotic arousal and the wish for potency and release.
- **Jung**: May be read as a compensatory or inflationary movement; freedom of spirit, but can warn of an ego detached from the ground (over-identification with height).
- **Post-Freudian**: Adler: a dream manufacturing an exaggerated sense of power/superiority to justify the life-style — note possible self-deception. Faraday: ask what waking situation this elevated feeling answers.
- **Chinese 周公解梦**: 《灵枢》『上盛则梦飞』——身体上部气盛之病梦;民间亦有飞升主升迁显达之吉解。八卦类象可接巽/天象。
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: Element of Air/Swords and Mercury: mind freed from the body, perspective and intellect; can warn of spiritual bypass (escaping Malkuth/the ground).
- **Dream Science**: Reported by ~50% (Nielsen) and disproportionately common in lucid dreams; associated with positive affect and vestibular/motor reactivation in REM; a frequent lucidity cue. No single validated 'meaning.'
- **Philosophy**: An ascending existential movement and a paradigm of imaginative freedom (Sartre): consciousness positing itself unbound from the real; Bachelard's aerial reverie of lightness.

## Teeth falling out / 牙齿脱落

- **Freud**: A castration symbol: 'the dream-work represents castration by baldness, hair-cutting, the loss of teeth, and beheading'; classically a punishment-fantasy for masturbation.
- **Jung**: Loss of grip/efficacy or a transition (shedding the old, as a child sheds teeth); compensation for anxiety about power, aging, or transformation.
- **Post-Freudian**: Hall/Van de Castle: code as a misfortune to the dreamer and track its rate vs. norms. Hartmann: read the accompanying emotion (helplessness, shame) as the Central Image's content.
- **Chinese 周公解梦**: 区分主体:『齿自落者父母凶』(主父母之忧);但『齿落更生子孙兴』(落而复生反主子孙兴旺)——同象因落/生而吉凶相反。
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: Loss of grip/potency or fear of transformation; Saturn (aging, limit) and the Death card (XIII) sense of inevitable change and shedding an old form — not literal death.
- **Dream Science**: Reported by ~1 in 5 (~19%; Nielsen). No validated universal meaning; some studies correlate it with dental irritation/bruxism (teeth-grinding) during sleep. Popular 'loss/anxiety' readings are low-evidence folk interpretation; best framed via continuity with explicit uncertainty.
- **Philosophy**: Approached skeptically: likely a residual bodily sensation amplified into image (Aristotle's phantasmata) before being any 'message'; hold symbolic readings as low-confidence.

## Being chased / pursued / 被追

- **Freud**: Read as anxiety produced by censorship: the pursuer often figures a disowned impulse or a feared punishment the wish is fleeing.
- **Jung**: A same-sex pursuer or dark intruder usually personifies the Shadow demanding recognition; the task is to turn and face, not flee.
- **Post-Freudian**: Gestalt/Perls & Faraday: become the pursuer — it is a disowned part seeking reintegration. Adler: a rehearsal of avoidance in the face of a current life task.
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: Often a Devil (XV) / shadow-bondage dynamic or a Tower upheaval being fled; framed as a phase pressing toward confrontation and release.
- **Dream Science**: The single most common typical-dream theme (~80% lifetime; Nielsen). Threat-simulation and content analysis treat it as rehearsal of avoidance and a marker of waking stress/anxiety — NOT an omen. Recurrence rises in stressful periods.
- **Philosophy**: On Revonsuo's threat-simulation reading, adaptive rehearsal of danger-response; phenomenologically the captive, will-resistant character of imaginary consciousness (Sartre).

## Nakedness / wrongly dressed in public / 裸露

- **Freud**: An exhibition dream rooted in infantile pleasure in nakedness; the embarrassment marks the censorship's later prohibition on the wish to be seen.
- **Jung**: Persona under examination — exposure of the true self beneath the social mask, or a misfit between role and identity.
- **Post-Freudian**: Gestalt: speak as the exposure to re-own it. Boss: how exposed/unsheltered is the dreamer's world right now? Hall/Van de Castle: code the social-evaluative content.
- **Dream Science**: A common embarrassment-type typical dream; content analysis ties it to waking self-consciousness, exposure, or social-evaluative concern (some propose mundane triggers like loose nightclothes). Reflects waking self-image concerns, not a fixed symbol.

## Helmet / armor / 盔甲 / 甲胄

- **Freud**: Headgear (hat, helmet) functions as a male/genital symbol; covering/removing it can carry castration or potency meanings. As armor it signals defense and the censorship's protective disguise.
- **Jung**: The persona as defensive shell, or a rigid identification that the psyche may be compensating; protective but also confining.
- **Chinese 周公解梦**: 『甲胄披身护吉利』——披挂铠甲主有护佑、得保护,吉象。
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: Court-card martial energy / the Chariot's (VII) controlled defense; Mars-toned protection and boundaried will.
- **Philosophy**: An analogon (Sartre) of a defended or role-bound self; phenomenologically a modification of the lived body's openness — protection that also encloses and limits.

## Dragon / 龙

- **Jung**: An archetypal beast — the chthonic guardian of the treasure-hard-to-attain; confronting or riding it images mastery of instinctual/numinous energy.
- **Chinese 周公解梦**: 大贵之象:『龙飞有官位大贵、乘龙入水有贵位、龙蛇入门主得财、妇人见龙生贵子、乘龙上山所求遂』。八卦类象→震(雷/龙)。
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: In the I Ching, the dragon of Hexagram 1 (Qian/The Creative) — ascending creative power through its line-stages ('hidden dragon', 'flying dragon').
- **Philosophy**: Resist fixed decoding; in the Chinese frame the dragon is a culturally loaded sign, so the philosophy lens flags how much meaning is convention vs. the dreamer's own felt resonance.

## Coffin / 棺 / 棺材

- **Freud**: A container/death image; via the box class it can carry female/womb symbolism, while the death scene may screen a wish concerning a family figure.
- **Jung**: Death/dying is rarely literal — the ending of an outworn attitude to permit transformation; the coffin holds what must be relinquished.
- **Chinese 周公解梦**: 谐音『官/财』多主吉:『将棺入宅禄位至、棺敛死人主得财』;但『开棺与死人言凶』为凶象。
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: Death (XIII) energy: radical transition, the ending of one phase; alchemical nigredo/mortificatio — dissolution preceding renewal.

## Blood / 血

- **Freud**: Can figure menstruation, defloration, or violence-tinged sexuality; affect attached to blood is often displaced from a charged latent thought.
- **Jung**: Life-force and sacrifice; the affect-charged substance of transformation, sometimes the wounding that precedes renewal.
- **Chinese 周公解梦**: 见血多主得财酒食:『杀人血污衣得财、刀伤出血主酒食、砍刺见血主大吉』。
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: The rubedo (reddening) and life-force/Mars; vital energy, sacrifice, or the coniunctio's quickening — intensification of life, not threat.

## Climbing high / ascending stairs / 登高 / 上山上楼

- **Freud**: 'Steep inclines, ladders and stairs, and going up or down them, are symbolic representations of the sexual act'; the rhythmic ascent and breathlessness reproduce coitus.
- **Jung**: Ascent toward consciousness or spirit; an upward movement in individuation, which compensation may counter if it is one-sided inflation.
- **Chinese 周公解梦**: 主富贵高升:『上楼阁坛俱大吉、上高堂大富贵至、乘龙上山所求遂』。
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: Climbing the Tree of Life: ascent through levels of consciousness from Malkuth (body) toward Kether (unity); the axis mundi linking below and above.
- **Philosophy**: An ascending existential trajectory (Binswanger/Foucault); a directed movement of the dreaming existence rather than a coded wish.

## Weeping / crying / 哭 / 哭泣

- **Jung**: Affect breaking through; often a compensation releasing what the waking ego holds in check, or grief for an attitude that must die.
- **Post-Freudian**: Hartmann: weeping marks the Central Image's dominant emotion (grief, helplessness). Gestalt: speak as the tears to own the disowned feeling.
- **Chinese 周公解梦**: 反兆为主:『与人哭泣有庆贺、放声大哭欢乐生』(哭反主喜);唯『床上哭泣主大凶』。
- **Dream Science**: Coded as an emotion (sadness) in content analysis; read through continuity as mirroring waking affect and via REM emotional-regulation ('overnight therapy').

## Mirror / reflection / 镜 / 照镜

- **Jung**: Confrontation with the Shadow or anima/animus; the reflected face poses the question of self-knowledge.
- **Chinese 周公解梦**: 以明暗断吉凶:『照镜明吉暗者凶』;又『拾得镜者招好妻、将镜自照远信至』。
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: The microcosm reflecting the macrocosm; 'as above, so below' made literal; confronting the shadow/anima in the glass.
- **Philosophy**: An instrument of the dream argument and self-modeling: is the reflected self the real one? In Vedanta it points toward the witnessing turīya behind every appearance.

## Knife / sword / blades / 刀剑

- **Freud**: 'All sharp and elongated weapons, knives, daggers, and pikes, represent the male member' — their thrust figures penetration and aggression.
- **Jung**: Discrimination and the cutting power of consciousness; the sword that separates, but also aggression demanding integration.
- **Chinese 周公解梦**: 多主吉利得财:『拔刀出行大吉利、剑在床头大吉利、持刀相杀见血吉』;唯『持刀刺人主失利』。
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: Suit of Swords / element Air: intellect, conflict, decision and the mind's clarity or cruelty.

## Bridge / crossing / 桥

- **Jung**: A transition between psychic states or life-stages; passage between conscious and unconscious territory.
- **Chinese 周公解梦**: 造修桥主和合禄位:『修桥梁者万事和、桥上坐立禄位至、新造桥者大和合』;另说『见渡桥者主厄散』(厄运消散)。
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: A path on the Tree of Life between Sephiroth; a rite of passage. I Ching: 'crossing the great water' — committing to a transition.
- **Philosophy**: Phenomenological liminality: a point where one mode of being passes into another; in dream-skepticism, the uncertain boundary between states.

## House / building / 房屋

- **Freud**: Houses represent the human body: smooth walls = men; walls with projections, ledges, and balconies one can grasp = women. A room generally represents a woman.
- **Jung**: The structure of the psyche/Self: upper floors = consciousness, cellar = personal unconscious, foundations = collective unconscious (Jung's own 'house dream').
- **Post-Freudian**: Gestalt: every room and object is a part of the self to speak as. Boss: read it as the spatial world the dreamer dwells in — how open, confined, or homelike is this existence?
- **Philosophy**: Bachelard's primal image of intimate space and the sheltered self; rooms map the psyche's topography — attic as rational/aspirational, cellar as obscure depths.

## Cellar / basement / cave / descent / 地窖 / 洞穴

- **Jung**: The personal-to-collective unconscious; descending is a willing entry into the psyche's depths to recover what is buried.
- **Post-Freudian**: Hillman: a literal entry into the Underworld — do not 'bring it up to the light'; stay with the descent as soul-making, not a problem to solve.
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: Descent to Malkuth/the unconscious; the alchemical vessel and the nigredo; The Hermit's (IX) inward retreat to find the inner light.
- **Philosophy**: Bachelard: the dark, irrational depth of the house-self; descent into what reflection cannot fully illuminate — not necessarily something repressed.

## Death / dying / corpse / 死亡

- **Freud**: Often screens a hostile infantile wish toward a relative, or, in late Freud, the death-drive and traumatic repetition rather than wish-fulfilment.
- **Jung**: Rarely literal; the ending of an outworn attitude or life-phase to permit transformation and rebirth.
- **Post-Freudian**: Hillman: a dead/absent person is an authentic Underworld figure to befriend, not a stand-in to decode. Boss: discloses how the dreamer's world is attuned to that relationship.
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: Death (XIII): radical transition, the ending of one phase; alchemical nigredo/mortificatio — dissolution before renewal, not physical death.

## Shadowy pursuer / dark intruder (same sex)

- **Jung**: The Shadow demanding recognition — what the ego has disowned is chasing it; the task is to turn and face.
- **Post-Freudian**: Gestalt/Perls & Faraday: become the intruder, a disowned part seeking reintegration.
- **Dream Science**: Coded as an aggressive social interaction; via threat-simulation and continuity, an expected stress-linked rehearsal rather than a fixed symbol.

## Alluring/haunting opposite-sex stranger

- **Freud**: A condensed object of desire; the figure fuses several real persons and an infantile prototype carrying the wish.
- **Jung**: Anima (in men) or Animus (in women) — the contrasexual soul-image and guide to the unconscious; its mood reveals one's relation to the inner depths.
- **Philosophy**: Read as an imaginative act positing an absent object (Sartre's analogon) rather than a literal acquaintance; status before symbolism.

## Mandala / circle / quaternity

- **Jung**: The Self and the ordering, healing drive toward wholeness; often emerges in disorientation as a self-regulating compensation.
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: The World (XXI) / squared circle; completion and integration at the end of the Fool's Journey.
- **Philosophy**: An image of the constructed self-model's wholeness; in Vedanta, a figure of the integrating witness across states.

## Divine child / infant / 婴孩

- **Freud**: Small children/vermin can figure siblings; a child of one's own may carry an infantile wish or a genital symbol.
- **Jung**: The nascent Self, new potential, the future personality being born; the 'child' archetype heralding individuation.
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: The Sun card's (XIX) child and the alchemical filius/divine child: rebirth and integrated innocence after the work.

## King / queen / royal couple / 帝后

- **Freud**: Emperor/Empress, King/Queen 'in most cases really represent the dreamer's parents' — royalty stands for father and mother.
- **Jung**: Union of opposites (conscious/unconscious, masculine/feminine); the alchemical coniunctio, an image of the Self and individuation's goal.
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: Sol and Luna, the coniunctio: union of opposites and integration of the Self; a royal wedding = inner wholeness.

## Tree / climbing a tree / 树

- **Freud**: Tree-trunks belong to the elongated-object class and figure the male member.
- **Jung**: The Self and the process of growth/individuation (the philosophical tree of alchemy); rootedness uniting underworld, earth, and sky.
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: The Tree of Life: ascent through levels of consciousness from Malkuth (roots/body) toward Kether (crown/unity).

## Sun / sunrise / bright light

- **Jung**: Consciousness, the ego's light, masculine/spiritual principle; in alchemy, Sol united with Luna in the coniunctio.
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: The Sun (XIX) and Sol: clarity, vitality, integrated joy, the rubedo of the work; the realized Self.
- **Philosophy**: Bachelard's reverie of well-being and self-gathering; in dream yoga, the 'natural light' of lucid presence.

## Moon / night / mist

- **Jung**: The unconscious, feminine, instinctual and cyclical principle (Luna); receptivity and the anima realm.
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: The Moon (XVIII) and Luna: the unconscious, illusion, dreams within dreams, the path of uncertainty before dawn.

## Gold / treasure / 金宝

- **Jung**: The Self and the 'treasure hard to attain' — the value won by confronting the unconscious; alchemy's goal projected.
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: The lapis/aurum of alchemy and Pentacles/Earth perfected: the realized Self; buried gold = unrealized potential.
- **Chinese 周公解梦**: 得金银珠宝多入财利之吉象,与『棺/血主财』同属取『富贵』之兆。

## Tower / building collapsing

- **Jung**: Collapse of a rigid conscious structure; a compensatory breakdown clearing ground for a new attitude.
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: The Tower (XVI): lightning-struck destruction of ego-structures and false beliefs; sudden revelation clearing ground for The Star.
- **Dream Science**: Coded as a misfortune/setting; read through continuity and threat-simulation as stress-linked, not prophetic.

## Storm / lightning

- **Jung**: A sudden irruption of unconscious energy or numinous insight overturning the conscious order.
- **Chinese 周公解梦**: 雷电风雨之象多关时令/感梦;八卦类象→震(雷)、巽(风),可作起卦取象。
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: Tower-lightning and Mars/Uranus: sudden disruptive insight; I Ching Hexagram 51 (The Arousing/Thunder) — shock that awakens.

## Devil / chains / bondage

- **Freud**: The devil/demon often personifies a repressed, forbidden (frequently sexual or hostile) impulse returning in fearful disguise.
- **Jung**: Bondage to a Shadow content or a compulsion; the dream stages what the ego is enslaved to but could face.
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: The Devil (XV): bondage to material desire, addiction, the shadow; Capricorn/Saturn. The chains are loose — the entrapment is chosen and releasable.

## Wheel / spinning / cycles / labyrinth

- **Jung**: The circular, circumambulating path of individuation around the Self; return that deepens rather than advances straight.
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: The Wheel of Fortune (X) and Jupiter: fate, cyclic change; a reminder the situation is in motion (like a moving-line hexagram).
- **Philosophy**: Spiral/labyrinth as an existential trajectory of disorientation (Binswanger/Foucault) and a dramatization of the dream-world's seamless immersion (Windt's ISTH).

## Locked door / key / threshold / 门

- **Freud**: A classic phallic-and-vaginal pairing: the key (penetrating) is male/phallic, the lock/keyhole female; locking/unlocking figures the sexual act.
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: The High Priestess (II) guarding hidden knowledge; an unopened Sephira/path. The key = the insight that unlocks the threshold.
- **Philosophy**: Phenomenological liminality: the uncertain boundary between states; the door marks where one world passes into another.

## Animal (general) / 走兽

- **Freud**: Small animals/vermin/insects represent small children or unwanted siblings; wild beasts can figure passionate or feared impulses.
- **Jung**: Instinct and the natural, somatic psyche; the species reveals which instinct (amplified mythologically). A wounded animal = neglected instinct.
- **Post-Freudian**: Lacan (Wolf Man): a signifier knotting the subject's fantasy — follow its associative/linguistic chain. Gestalt: embody the animal to recover disowned energy.
- **Chinese 周公解梦**: 走兽鸟虫各有专条断验(如蛇主贵子、龙主大贵),按类象/谐音取义断吉凶。

## Boxes / chests / vessels / 箱箧

- **Freud**: Female/womb symbols: 'small boxes, chests, cupboards, and ovens correspond to the female organ; also cavities, ships, and all kinds of vessels.'
- **Jung**: A container of contents not yet conscious; what is held, hidden, or about to be opened in the psyche.
- **Philosophy**: Bachelard's drawer/chest: an image of intimate, sheltered memory and secrecy — topology of the self's interiority, not a sexual cipher.

## Necktie / hat / hanging object

- **Freud**: 'In the dreams of men, one often finds the necktie as a symbol for the penis' — a hanging, elongated, wearable object in the phallic class.
- **Jung**: A persona/appearance element; how the dreamer presents or constrains a social role.

## Butterfly / 蝴蝶

- **Jung**: The psyche/soul (psyche = butterfly) and metamorphosis; an image of transformation toward the Self.
- **Chinese 周公解梦**: 象梦取义,亦可关蜕变/喜兆;在中国语境更常引《庄子》梦蝶之喻而非吉凶查条。
- **Philosophy**: The Zhuangzi emblem of porous identity and the transformation of things (物化): a sign the waking/dreaming and self/other boundaries are being questioned — carry it lightly, with delight.

## Tidal wave / tsunami / overwhelming wave

- **Jung**: The unconscious erupting to overwhelm the ego; a compensatory flood when the conscious attitude is too rigid or dry.
- **Post-Freudian**: Hartmann's paradigmatic Central Image of being overwhelmed — terror or helplessness, common after trauma (documented after 9/11). A picture of emotion, not prediction.
- **Dream Science**: Read via the overnight-therapy/affect-regulation literature and continuity: a vivid image of waking distress being reprocessed, not an omen.

## Injection / medical/bodily image

- **Freud**: In the Irma dream, a bodily intervention condensing guilt, wish, and self-exculpation around the dreamer's professional anxieties.
- **Post-Freudian**: Lacan (Dream of Irma): a point where the dream presses toward the Real — something 'in front of which words stop'; attend to the breakdown of sense, not a tidy symbol.
- **Dream Science**: Possible incorporation of bodily/somatic sensation into dream content; coded neutrally, read for continuity with waking health concerns.

## Wise old man / old woman

- **Jung**: Archetype of meaning/spirit (mana personality) — guidance from the deeper psyche; risk of inflation if the ego identifies with it.
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: The Hermit (IX) or Hierophant (V): inner guidance, accumulated wisdom, the teacher on the journey.

## Double / doppelgänger / mirror-self

- **Jung**: The Shadow or an unintegrated aspect of the personality met face to face.
- **Philosophy**: A confrontation with the constructed self-model (Metzinger): the dream externalizes that the 'I' is itself a representation; Zhuangzi's question of which one dreams the other.

## False awakening / waking inside a dream

- **Dream Science**: A documented dream phenomenon and frequent precursor/cue to lucidity (reality-testing failures); not a portent.
- **Philosophy**: The dream argument made vivid: experiential evidence that waking cannot be certified from within; an opening toward lucidity (dream yoga) and the witness (turīya).

## Lion / wild beast

- **Jung**: Raw instinctual power; the beast to be related to rather than slain — neglected or untamed energy.
- **Divination (Tarot/I-Ching/Esoteric)**: Strength (VIII) and Leo/Sun: instinctual power tamed by gentle will; the alchemical 'green lion' devouring the sun (raw matter to be refined).

## Darkness / void / imageless state

- **Jung**: The unconscious not yet given form, or the prima materia before differentiation; potential rather than absence.
- **Dream Science**: May correspond to near-imageless NREM or 'white dream' states; the posterior hot zone's low activity, not a meaningful symbol.
- **Philosophy**: Near the minimal end of Windt's spectrum, pointing to dreamless-sleep-like states; in Vedanta, the threshold of suṣupti rather than an absence of meaning.
