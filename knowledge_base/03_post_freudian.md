# Post-Freudian & depth-psychology dream schools (Adler, Hillman/archetypal, Boss/Daseinsanalysis, Lacan, Gestalt/Perls, Hall content analysis, Faraday, Hartmann)

This domain gathers the major dream traditions that grew up *after* (and often *against*) Freud and Jung, giving the yume app a pluralist toolkit rather than a single decoding scheme. They split along a few fault lines.

**Teleological vs. archaeological.** Where Freud reads dreams backward (toward repressed childhood wishes), Alfred Adler reads them *forward*. For Adler the dream is a rehearsal of the dreamer's "style of life," a self-generated mood or emotion that prepares (and often self-deceptively over-prepares) the person to meet a current problem in line with their goal of fitness/superiority. The dream produces the feeling the waking person needs to justify the line they were already taking.

**Image vs. translation.** James Hillman's archetypal/imaginal psychology is the strongest anti-interpretive stance: "befriend the image, do not translate it." Dreams belong to the Underworld (Hades/psyche), are complete in themselves, and should be *stuck with* rather than dragged up into ego daylight. The app should let users dwell with, personify, and stay close to an image rather than swap it for a "meaning."

**Existential disclosure.** Medard Boss, fusing Freud's clinic with Heidegger, treats the dream as a genuine mode of *being-in-the-world*: it discloses how the dreamer's existence is currently open or closed, attuned, related. There is no hidden latent content behind a manifest mask — the dream shows the dreamer's world directly, just as waking life does. Boss deliberately refuses the Freudian "unconscious as storehouse."

**Structural/linguistic.** Lacan returns to Freud but rereads the dream-work (condensation, displacement) as the rhetoric of an unconscious "structured like a language" — condensation = metaphor, displacement = metonymy. The famous Dream of Irma's Injection stages the subject's confrontation with the Real (the point where words stop), and the Wolf Man shows the signifier organizing fantasy. The dream is a text addressed to the Other, not a private symbol-code.

**Self-ownership.** Fritz Perls' Gestalt approach holds that *every* element of a dream — people, objects, settings — is a projected, disowned fragment of the self. Healing comes by re-identifying with each part, classically by speaking *as* each element (empty-chair / two-chair work), reintegrating what was alienated. Ann Faraday popularized a practical, do-it-yourself version: drop dream dictionaries, only the dreamer can read their own dream language, and use Gestalt-style identification ("become the thing") plus checking the dream against waking life.

**Empirical/quantitative.** Calvin Hall and Robert Van de Castle built a content-analysis system that sidesteps interpretation entirely: code dreams into nominal categories (characters, social/aggressive/friendly/sexual interactions, misfortunes, fortunes, emotions, settings, objects), compute rates and ratios, and compare against published norms. This is the empirical, reliable backbone for any app that aggregates dream data over time.

**Contemporary neuro-affective.** Ernest Hartmann's Contemporary Theory of Dreaming says dreaming makes broad *connections* guided by the dreamer's dominant emotion; the dream's Central Image (CI), and its intensity, picture that emotion (e.g., the tidal-wave dream of terror). His boundaries construct (thin vs. thick personalities) predicts who recalls vivid dreams and suffers nightmares, and Image Rehearsal Therapy (rescripting nightmares) is the clinical payoff.

**How the app should "read" a dream:** offer parallel lenses, not one verdict. Adler asks "what is this rehearsing, and toward what goal?"; Hillman says "stay with the image"; Boss asks "what world is disclosed here?"; Lacan tracks wordplay and address; Gestalt/Faraday invites "become each part"; Hall/Van de Castle quietly tallies content over many dreams; Hartmann surfaces the Central Image and its emotion. yume can present a dream through whichever lens the user picks, and use Hall/Van de Castle as the neutral statistical layer underneath.


## 经典著作 / Canonical Works

### The Dream and the Underworld

- **作者**: James Hillman  **年份**: 1979
- **核心概念**: Befriend the image, do not translate it, Dreams belong to the Underworld (Hades), not daylight ego, Imaginal reduction to mythic/archetypal background, Dreams are complete in themselves — not Freudian wish nor Jungian compensation
- **为何重要**: The canonical anti-interpretive dream stance; grounds an app mode that lets users dwell with and personify images instead of decoding them.
- **合法全文**: 🔴 在版/无免费全文 — HarperCollins (ISBN 9780060906825); summaries on Goodreads https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/33026.The_Dream_and_the_Underworld

### The Interpretation of Dreams (Die Traumdeutung)

- **作者**: Sigmund Freud (Brill translation)  **年份**: 1900 (1913 Eng.)
- **核心概念**: Manifest vs. latent content, Dream-work: condensation, displacement, secondary revision, Dream as disguised wish-fulfillment
- **为何重要**: The shared reference point every post-Freudian school revises or rejects; the 1913 Brill translation is genuinely public domain and free to quote in full.
- **合法全文**: 🟢 公共领域 — Project Gutenberg (1913 Brill translation, eBook #66048) https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/66048

### The Content Analysis of Dreams

- **作者**: Calvin S. Hall & Robert L. Van de Castle  **年份**: 1966
- **核心概念**: Nominal coding categories (characters, interactions, emotions, settings, objects), Content indicators and ratios (e.g., aggression/friendliness), Normative tables for men and women, Reliability via perfect-agreement coding
- **为何重要**: The empirical backbone for quantifying dream content over many entries; the full coding rules and norms are openly published online and ideal for app analytics.
- **合法全文**: 🟢 开放获取 — DreamBank / UCSC (Domhoff & Schneider) — full coding rules, categories, and normative tables https://dreams.ucsc.edu/Coding/

### The Nature and Functions of Dreaming

- **作者**: Ernest Hartmann  **年份**: 2011
- **核心概念**: Contemporary Theory of Dreaming: dreaming makes broad connections guided by dominant emotion, Central Image (CI) and CI intensity picture the emotion, Thin vs. thick boundaries personality dimension, Nightmares, trauma dreams, and Image Rehearsal Therapy
- **为何重要**: The leading contemporary neuro-affective model; supplies the Central-Image and boundaries features and an evidence-based nightmare (IRT) pathway. Many of Hartmann's papers are free PDFs on his estate site.
- **合法全文**: 🔴 在版/无免费全文 — Oxford University Press (book); free related papers at ernesthartmann.com https://ernesthartmann.com/files/The-Central-Image-makes-Big-Dreams-big_2008_Dreaming%20_1_.pdf

### Gestalt Therapy Verbatim

- **作者**: Frederick (Fritz) Perls  **年份**: 1969
- **核心概念**: Every dream element is a disowned/projected part of the self, Re-identify with each part by speaking as it (empty-chair / two-chair), Healing = reintegration of alienated fragments, Dreamwork done live in seminar (Esalen) rather than via fixed symbols
- **为何重要**: Source of the 'become each part of your dream' technique that powers an interactive, role-play dream mode.
- **合法全文**: 🔴 在版/无免费全文 — Real People Press / Bantam (ISBN via Goodreads listing) https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/402226.Gestalt_Therapy_Verbatim

### The Dream Game

- **作者**: Ann Faraday  **年份**: 1974
- **核心概念**: Only the dreamer can decode their own dream language, Reject dream dictionaries, Gestalt-style identification ('become the object'), Check dream content against waking-life situations
- **为何重要**: The practical, popular bridge that turns Gestalt/humanistic dreamwork into self-help steps — directly templatable into guided app exercises.
- **合法全文**: 🔴 在版/无免费全文 — Harper & Row / Penguin (also Dream Power, 1972) https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ann_Faraday

### The Seminar of Jacques Lacan, Book II (incl. the Dream of Irma's Injection)

- **作者**: Jacques Lacan (ed. J.-A. Miller)  **年份**: 1954–55 (Eng. 1988)
- **核心概念**: Unconscious structured like a language, Condensation = metaphor, displacement = metonymy, Dream of Irma's Injection as confrontation with the Real, Dream as addressed to the Other, not a private symbol-code
- **为何重要**: Provides the structural/linguistic lens — tracking wordplay, signifiers, and address — distinct from any symbol lookup.
- **合法全文**: 🔴 在版/无免费全文 — W. W. Norton; detailed open commentary at LacanOnline https://www.lacanonline.com/2010/05/reading-seminar-ii-chapter-xiii-the-dream-of-irmas-injection/


## 核心概念 / Key Concepts

- **Style of life (Lebensstil)** — Adler's term for the individual's unified, goal-directed pattern of meeting life; dreams sketch and rehearse it.
  - *用于解梦*: Tag a dream with the life-problem it seems to be rehearsing and the self-image/goal it props up — and flag the Adlerian 'self-deception' where the dream over-inflates the dreamer's power.
- **Befriend the image** — Hillman's instruction to stay with a dream image, personify and dwell in it, rather than translate it into a concept.
  - *用于解梦*: Offer a 'stay with the image' mode: prompts to describe, address, and live alongside an image with no interpretation button.
- **Imaginal / soul-making** — Hillman's view of psyche as fundamentally image-making; dreams relate to mythic (often Greek) archetypal backgrounds.
  - *用于解梦*: Optionally link a vivid figure to a mythic resonance as enrichment, framed as analogy, never as 'the meaning.'
- **Dasein / being-in-the-world** — Boss/Heidegger: humans exist by already being involved in a meaningful world; the dream discloses the state of that involvement.
  - *用于解梦*: Ask phenomenological questions: how open or closed, attuned, related is the dreamer's world in this dream? Avoid hidden-meaning prompts.
- **Unconscious structured like a language** — Lacan's thesis that dream-work follows linguistic laws — condensation as metaphor, displacement as metonymy.
  - *用于解梦*: Surface puns, homophones, repeated words and 'who is this addressed to?' rather than fixed symbol meanings.
- **Disowned part / projection** — Perls/Gestalt: each dream element is an alienated fragment of the self awaiting reintegration.
  - *用于解梦*: Interactive 'speak as each element' exercise (empty-chair style) to re-own projected parts.
- **Content indicators & norms** — Hall/Van de Castle nominal categories and ratios scored against published male/female norms.
  - *用于解梦*: Quantitative analytics layer: auto-code characters, interactions, emotions, settings; show trends and norm comparisons over a dream journal.
- **Central Image (CI)** — Hartmann's striking, emotionally powerful image at a dream's core whose intensity tracks the dreamer's dominant emotion.
  - *用于解梦*: Detect and rate the dream's Central Image and infer the underlying emotion (e.g., terror, helplessness, guilt).
- **Thin vs. thick boundaries** — Hartmann's personality dimension; thin-boundary people recall more, more vivid dreams and more nightmares.
  - *用于解梦*: Optional boundaries self-assessment that personalizes recall expectations and nightmare-support features.
- **Image Rehearsal Therapy (IRT)** — Evidence-based nightmare treatment: rewrite the nightmare's script while awake and mentally rehearse the new version.
  - *用于解梦*: Guided nightmare-rescripting flow for recurrent bad dreams, with a clear 'not medical treatment' disclaimer.

## 符号映射 / Symbol Mappings

- **Falling**: Hartmann: a Central Image often expressing the emotion of vulnerability/loss of control. Adler: may dramatize a feared loss of status/superiority within the life-style. Gestalt: identify with the falling itself ('I am falling') to own the surrendered control.
- **Tidal wave / tsunami**: Hartmann's paradigmatic Central Image of being overwhelmed — terror or helplessness, common after trauma and documented after 9/11. Treat as a picture of emotion, not prediction.
- **Being chased / pursued**: Gestalt/Perls & Faraday: become the pursuer — it is a disowned part of you seeking reintegration. Adler: a rehearsal of avoidance in the face of a current life task.
- **Descent / going underground, basement, cave**: Hillman: literal entry into the Underworld — do not 'bring it up to the light'; stay with the descent as soul-making, not as a problem to solve.
- **House / building**: Gestalt: every room and object is a part of the self to speak as. Boss: read it as the spatial world the dreamer dwells in — how open, confined, or homelike is this existence right now?
- **A dead or absent person**: Hillman: an authentic Underworld figure/psychic image to befriend, not a stand-in to decode. Boss: discloses how the dreamer's world is presently attuned to that relationship.
- **Injection / medical/bodily image**: Lacan (Dream of Irma): a point where the dream presses toward the Real — something 'in front of which words stop'; attend to the breakdown of sense, not a tidy symbol.
- **Animals (e.g., wolves)**: Lacan (Wolf Man): a signifier knotting the subject's fantasy — follow its associative/linguistic chain. Gestalt: embody the animal to recover the disowned instinct/energy.
- **Teeth falling out**: Hall/Van de Castle: code as a misfortune to the dreamer (track its rate vs. norms) rather than assign fixed meaning. Hartmann: read the accompanying emotion (helplessness, shame) as the Central Image's content.
- **Flying / soaring**: Adler: a dream that manufactures an exaggerated sense of power/superiority to justify the life-style — note the possible self-deception. Faraday: ask what waking situation this elevated feeling is responding to.

## 作为辩论 Agent 的人设 / App Integration

Design yume as a multi-lens reader, not a symbol dictionary (which Faraday and Hillman both explicitly reject). Recommended modes: (1) 'Stay with the image' (Hillman) — no interpret button, just personify/describe/dwell; (2) 'Become each part' (Perls/Faraday) — interactive empty-chair role-play where the user speaks as dream elements; (3) 'Rehearsal' lens (Adler) — prompt for the current life-problem and the self-image the dream props up, with a gentle self-deception check; (4) 'Disclosed world' lens (Boss) — phenomenological prompts about openness/attunement/relatedness; (5) 'Language' lens (Lacan) — auto-detect puns, repeated words, homophones, and 'who is this for?'; (6) analytics layer (Hall/Van de Castle) — auto-code characters, interactions, emotions, settings/objects and chart trends vs. published norms over a journal; (7) emotion/Central-Image detector + optional thin/thick boundaries profile (Hartmann). Build a dedicated, clearly non-clinical nightmare flow using IRT-style rescripting with a visible disclaimer and crisis-resource fallback. Legal/content: ship full quotable text only for genuinely free sources — Freud (Gutenberg #66048, public domain) and the Hall/Van de Castle coding rules + norms (dreams.ucsc.edu, open access); link out (do not reproduce) Hillman, Boss, Perls, Faraday, Lacan, and Hartmann's book, which remain in copyright, while Hartmann's individual papers can be linked as free PDFs at ernesthartmann.com. Let users pick a 'school' lens per dream and clearly label every reading as one perspective among several, never a verdict.
